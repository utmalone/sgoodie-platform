# Jenn Verrier UX/UI Reference (Captured 2026-02-03)

## Scope reviewed
- https://www.jennverrier.com/
- https://www.jennverrier.com/architectural-photography-portfolio
- https://www.jennverrier.com/architectural-photography-portfolio/lanvale-midcentury-interiors
- https://www.jennverrier.com/about-jenn-verrier-photography
- https://www.jennverrier.com/journal-architectural-photography
- https://www.jennverrier.com/contact-jenn-verrier

## Global layout and visual tone
- Clean, minimal, white background with black or charcoal text.
- Generous whitespace and breathing room between sections.
- Text is left-aligned and set in short, readable line lengths.
- Large editorial headings paired with small uppercase section labels.
- Photography is the hero. Images dominate page height and width.

## Header and navigation
- Primary nav items: Work, About, Journal, Contact.
- Brand text: "Jenn Verrier" appears in the header.
- Social icons for Instagram and LinkedIn appear in the header.
- Mobile or compact layout shows an "Open Menu / Close Menu" toggle.
- A "Skip to Content" link is present before the header.

## Footer
- Contact block with phone number and email.
- Instagram handle link.
- Copyright line and design credit.
- Location or availability line (DC, MD, VA, PA, NY).

## Home page
- Hero text stack with two large lines:
  - "Architectural + Interiors PhotograpHER"
  - "Mid-Atlantic / Northeast"
- Short mission statement under the hero.
- Large image grid that fills the page; images appear in a masonry-style collection.
- Each image has a "View fullsize" action that opens or enlarges the photo.
- A "view projects" link appears below the image grid.

## Work (portfolio index)
- A minimal, text-only list of project names.
- Project names are displayed as large, clickable links.
- Some items are labeled "Coming Soon" or "Draft".
- No descriptive text or imagery is shown on this index page.

## Project detail pages (example: Lanvale)
- Starts with a large hero image.
- Small label with the project name followed by a large headline.
- Alternating sequence of images and short text sections.
- Text sections use short headings and one to two paragraph descriptions.
- A compact metadata block at the end with location, designer, stylist, photographer, and year.
- "Previous" and "Next" links at the bottom for pagination between projects.

## About page
- Large hero image at top.
- Small section label "about" followed by a large headline.
- Several paragraphs describing the photographer and approach.
- "Approach & results" section with multiple sub-cards:
  - Each sub-card includes an image, a short title, and a short description.
- "Featured in" list of publications.
- Personal bio section with headshot and a "get in touch" link.

## Journal index
- Small label "journal" and headline "latest shoot stories".
- List of posts in a repeating card or list pattern:
  - Category (Project Feature, Roundup, etc.), author, date.
  - Post title, short excerpt, and "Read More" link.
- "Older Posts" link at the bottom.

## Journal post pages (example)
- Post title and category at top.
- Several large images with "View fullsize".
- Body paragraph describing the shoot.
- Credits block at the end with client or project details.

## Contact page
- Large hero image.
- Small label "contact" and headline "discussion + booking".
- Short paragraph prompting a form submission with project details.
- Contact details: business name, email, phone.
- Social links and an Instagram grid or row.

## Interaction notes
- Primary actions are subtle text links; imagery is the main call to action.
- Image interactions emphasize lightbox or fullsize viewing.
- Navigation is consistent across pages.
